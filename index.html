Brendan Fraser

Hollywood's Finest Leading Man

Here is a timeline of Brendan Fraser's life:

  • 1968 - Born in Indianapolis, Indiana.
  • 1990 - Graduated from Seattle's Cornish College of the Arts.
  • 1991 - Made his film debut as a sailor headed to Vietman in the movie Dogfight.
  • 1992 - Got his first leading film role in the comedy film Encino Man where he played a frozen pre-historic caveman who is thawed out in the present day.
  • 1997 - Had his first major box office success with the family comedy film George of the Jungle.
  • 1999 - Universal Studios releases one of the greatest and most beloved cinematic masterpieces of the modern era, The Mummy, starring Fraser with co-star Rachel Weisz.
  • 2001 - Universal Studios releases the sequel to The Mummy, titled The Mummy Returns. It is widely regarded to be one of the best movie sequels released in the year 2001.
  • 1999-2008 - Fraser stars in several other movies, including Dudley Do-Right, Monkey Bone, and Journey to the Center of the Earth, none of which were particularly memorable.
  • 2006 - Becomes the first American-born actor to be inducted into Canada's Walk of Fame. (Frazer holds dual American and Canadian citizenship.)
  • 2016 - Visits Mumbai for two weeks to film his first Bollywood film role for the movie Line of Descent.
  • 2018 - In the wake of the worldwide #MeToo movement against sexual harassment and abuse, Fraser alleges that he was sexually assaulted by Philip Berk, the president of the Hollywood Foreign Press Association, at a luncheon in the summer of 2003. The assault, his subsequent divorce, and the death of his mother launched Fraser into a depression that, combined with his health issues and a backlash within the industry over speaking out against Berk, he believes caused his career to decline.
  • 2021 and Beyond - In January 2021, Fraser is announced as part of the cast of Darren Aronofsky's upcoming film The Whale. In August of the same year, Fraser was announced as part of the cast of Martin Scorsese's upcoming film Killers of the Flower Moon, as well as Max Barbakow's upcoming comedy film Brothers. The "Brenaissance" has begun.
